# Review Round 1 -- Rural Single-Facility Administrator

**Persona:** Karen Hofstetter, Administrator of a 45-bed SNF in small-town Iowa. Solo handles marketing, compliance, and admissions. 6 Google reviews total (3 are old 1-stars). No prior reputation management tool experience. Found the site by Googling "how to get more nursing home reviews." Skeptical about spending $149/month on anything.

---

## 1. Overwhelm Level

Honestly? It is a lot. I scrolled past the comparison table entirely -- I do not know who Podium or BirdEye are, and I do not care. That section feels like it was written for someone who is already shopping between tools. I am not shopping. I am trying to figure out if this whole category of product is even for me.

The features section lists six things and I cannot tell which ones I would actually use on the Basic plan. "F-Tag & Survey Integration" and "Review Velocity Analytics" sound like things a corporate chain needs, not a 45-bed facility where I am the only person who would log into this. I started skimming by feature number four.

The page is not too techy in a software sense -- no code or dashboards shown -- but it is too techy in a "healthcare conference vendor booth" sense. It assumes I already know I need reputation management. I do not. I just want more reviews and fewer bad ones at the top of my Google page.

## 2. Price Sensitivity

$149/month is $1,788 a year. I would have to justify that to ownership with zero proof it works for a facility my size. Every testimonial is from facilities 2-3 times bigger than mine (90-bed, 120-bed, multi-facility group). None of them look like me.

Bigger problem: the Basic plan at $149 only includes email campaigns, not SMS. The entire pitch -- the hero, the How It Works section, the testimonials -- is built around SMS. So the thing that convinced me to keep reading is not included in the tier I would actually buy. That feels like a bait and switch. By the time I see that SMS requires $300/month (Standard), I am done. $3,600/year for a 45-bed facility in Iowa is not happening.

The pricing section highlights Standard as "Most Popular." That tells me this product is not really for me. It is for regional operators. I am not the customer you are featuring, so why would I trust that the Basic plan gets any attention?

## 3. Setup Anxiety

"Upload your discharge list" in Step 1 immediately raises questions nobody answers:

- Who makes this CSV? Me? My MDS coordinator who is already stretched thin?
- How often do I have to do this? Every week? After every discharge?
- What if I do not use PointClickCare? (I do not. We use MatrixCare.) The whole page talks about PCC like it is the only EHR that exists.
- "Client-side parser strips all PHI" -- I do not know what client-side means and I do not know how to verify that claim to my compliance officer.

The page says "under a minute of setup time" but that only describes sending, not the extraction, not the configuration, not connecting my Google Business Profile. What does setup actually look like from start to finish? A 2-minute walkthrough video would do more for me than all six feature cards combined.

## 4. Trust

Three concerns:

**No proof these testimonials are real.** Rebecca T., David N., Maria S. -- first name plus last initial, no facility name, no photo. These could be made up. If this product is about earning trust through reviews, the website itself should model that. Link to actual Google reviews of Caresite, or show real facility names with permission.

**No company information.** Who runs this? How long have you been around? How many facilities use you? The footer says "Caresite.io. All rights reserved" and links to a privacy policy and terms of service (which I assume do not exist yet since this is a new site). There is no About page, no team page, no physical address, no phone number. I am handing you access to my families' phone numbers -- I need to know who you are.

**"No BAA required" makes me nervous, not reassured.** You are telling me no BAA is needed because you never touch PHI. But I am uploading a discharge list with family names and phone numbers. Whether or not that meets the legal definition of PHI, my compliance officer will want to see something in writing. Saying "no BAA required" sounds like "we do not want to sign one," not "we have thought this through."

## 5. Jargon Check

Terms I had to stop and think about or did not understand:

- **Sentiment gating** -- I figured it out from the FAQ but it took extra clicks. The features section uses this term before explaining it. Call it what it is: "happy families go to Google, unhappy families come to you privately." That is one sentence and I immediately get it.
- **PCC / PointClickCare** -- I know what it is but I do not use it. Seeing it everywhere makes me feel like this product is not compatible with my facility.
- **TCPA opt-out** -- I have no idea what TCPA is. (It is the Telephone Consumer Protection Act, but you should not assume I know that.)
- **F-tag & survey correlation** -- I know what F-tags are and I know what surveys are but "correlation reports" sounds like something an analytics team would use. I do not have an analytics team.
- **Review velocity** -- Just say "how many reviews you are getting."
- **Client-side parser** -- Means nothing to me. Say "your data is cleaned on your own computer before anything is sent to us."
- **HIPAA-safe by architecture** -- I understand HIPAA. I do not understand what "by architecture" adds. It sounds like marketing language for something you should just prove.

## 6. Would I Fill Out the Form?

Not today. Here is what is stopping me and what would tip me over:

**What is stopping me:**
- I do not know if this works for a facility my size with my budget.
- The real product (SMS campaigns) costs twice what the headline price suggests.
- I cannot tell who is behind this company.
- I do not use PointClickCare and the site makes me think I need to.

**What would tip me over:**
- A case study from a small facility (under 60 beds, single location, rural market) showing before/after review counts and rating.
- A clear statement that the Basic plan works without SMS -- or better, include SMS in Basic and charge what you need to charge. Do not bury the real price.
- A phone number. I am not filling out a form and waiting "one business day" for someone I have never heard of. Let me call. Or at minimum, offer a live chat.
- The form asks for my name, email, and facility name. It does not ask how many beds I have, what EHR I use, or what my current Google rating is. If you asked those questions, I would feel like you are actually going to tailor the conversation to me instead of giving me a generic sales pitch.

## 7. Top 5 Specific Changes (ranked)

### 1. Fix the pricing bait-and-switch around SMS

The entire page sells SMS-driven review campaigns. The hero says "SMS campaigns that turn discharged families into five-star advocates." How It Works says "Personalized SMS messages are sent to responsible parties." Then the pricing section reveals SMS is not in the $149 Basic plan -- it is only in the $300 Standard plan. Either include SMS in Basic (even if that means raising Basic to $199) or stop leading with SMS in the hero and How It Works sections. Right now the page promises something the entry-level plan does not deliver.

### 2. Add a testimonial and/or case study from a small, single-location facility

Every testimonial is from a large or multi-facility operation (120-bed Ohio, multi-facility Texas, 90-bed Florida). A 45-bed rural administrator sees zero social proof that this product works at her scale. Add at least one testimonial from a facility under 60 beds, ideally single-location, ideally in a rural or small-town market. If you do not have one yet, that is a sign the product needs to prove itself in that segment before marketing to it.

### 3. Add basic company credibility signals

There is no About page, no team bios, no founding story, no customer count, no physical address, no phone number. For an administrator being asked to trust a startup with her families' contact information and her facility's online reputation, this is a dealbreaker. At minimum add: who founded this and why, how many facilities are currently using it, and a phone number or live chat option in the contact section.

### 4. Reduce jargon and explain things in plain language the first time they appear

Replace "sentiment gating" with a plain English description ("routes happy families to Google and unhappy families to a private form") the first time it appears in the features section. Spell out TCPA on first use. Replace "client-side parser" with "your data is cleaned on your computer before it reaches us." Replace "review velocity" with "review volume" or "how many reviews you are getting." The FAQ does a decent job explaining things -- the problem is the features section uses jargon before the FAQ has a chance to define it.

### 5. Address EHR compatibility beyond PointClickCare

The page mentions PointClickCare seven times and no other EHR system. A huge share of small SNFs use MatrixCare, American HealthTech, or Netsmart. If the CSV upload works regardless of EHR, say that clearly: "Works with any EHR that can export a discharge list -- PointClickCare, MatrixCare, Netsmart, or even a manual spreadsheet." If PCC is the only integration, be honest about that too, but make it clear the product still works without it.

---

**Bottom line:** The page is well-designed and clearly written for the mid-market SNF operator who already knows they need reputation management. It does not speak to the small-facility administrator who is Googling "how to get more nursing home reviews" for the first time. That person needs smaller proof points, plainer language, a lower entry price that includes what the page is actually selling, and real human credibility signals before she will hand over her contact information.
