# Round 2 Review: Diane Marshall

**Reviewer:** Diane Marshall, Administrator, 180-bed SNF, Cleveland, OH
**Experience:** 22 years in post-acute care
**Round 1 date:** Prior review cycle
**Round 2 date:** Current review cycle
**Focus:** Evaluating changes made in response to Round 1 feedback

---

## 1. What Improved

**1a. EHR language is no longer PCC-only.** This was one of my biggest concerns in Round 1. The trust bar now reads "Works with PointClickCare, MatrixCare & all EHRs," the HowItWorks step says "Export a CSV from PointClickCare or your EHR," and the FAQ explicitly names PointClickCare, MatrixCare, Netsmart, and AHT with a clear explanation that any EHR with CSV export works. My MatrixCare question is answered. This is a meaningful fix.

**1b. The CTA mismatch is resolved.** Round 1 flagged that "Start Your Free Trial" was vague and did not match the value being offered. The CTAs now consistently say "Book Your Free Audit" across the hero, the mid-page banner, and the pricing cards. The contact section headline ("See what 10 reviews a month looks like") is specific and compelling. The audit framing — "We'll walk through your current Google profile, show you where you stand against nearby facilities, and map out a 90-day review growth plan" — gives me a concrete reason to fill out the form. This is a significant improvement.

**1c. The contact form is substantially better.** Round 1 said the form asked for almost nothing and felt unqualified. The form now collects name, email, facility name, phone (optional), role (with SNF-specific dropdown options including Administrator, DON, Regional Operations), and number of facilities. This tells me the sales team will know who I am before the first call. The role dropdown also signals that Caresite knows who buys this product.

**1d. The pricing/SMS mismatch is fixed.** Round 1 called out that the hero sold SMS but the Basic plan was email-only. The Basic plan now includes "SMS + email review campaigns." That removes the bait-and-switch feeling entirely. The pricing tiers make sense now — Basic gives you the core experience, Standard adds volume and multi-location, Enterprise adds integrations and compliance reporting.

**1e. A phone number is visible.** Round 1 asked for a phone number somewhere on the page. It now appears in the trust bar and the footer — (435) 210-6111. That is a small detail that matters to administrators my age. I can call someone.

---

## 2. Still Missing

**2a. No company credibility section.** This was my number one issue in Round 1 and it has not been addressed. There is still no About section, no team names, no founding story, no customer count, no company age, no advisory board. The footer says "Caresite.io. All rights reserved" and that is the extent of the company identity. I still do not know if this is two people in a garage or a funded team with a support organization. For a healthcare buyer connecting discharge workflows to a platform, this remains a dealbreaker. I said this was non-negotiable last time and I mean it.

**2b. Testimonials are still anonymous.** The same three testimonials from Round 1 — Rebecca T., David N., Maria S. — are still present with no facility names, no verifiable details. "120-bed SNF, Ohio" is not something I can check. I asked for at least one named facility or recognizable group. If you have real customers, let them be named. If you do not have real customers yet, say you are in pilot and show the pilot data instead — honesty would actually build more trust than fabricated-looking anonymized quotes.

**2c. No product screenshots or dashboard visuals.** The analytics dashboard, the dispute assistant interface, the SMS campaign builder, the sentiment gating flow — none of these are shown anywhere on the page. I am being asked to pay $149 to $590 per month for software I have never seen. Every competitor I have evaluated shows product screenshots. The case study section would be an ideal place for a before/after Google Business Profile screenshot. The features section describes six capabilities with zero visual evidence that they exist.

**2d. Privacy Policy and Terms of Service still appear to be placeholder links.** The footer links to /privacy and /terms. If those pages do not exist, this is the same issue I flagged in Round 1. A healthcare buyer clicking those links and getting a 404 will close the tab.

---

## 3. New Issues

**3a. The case study is still not verifiable.** A case study section was added, which is good — "From 2.8 stars to 4.4 in 90 days" with a 120-bed facility in the Pacific Northwest. But it has no facility name, no administrator quote attached to it, and no visual proof (screenshot of the actual Google profile). This reads as a marketing claim, not a case study. A real case study has a name attached. If the facility will not be named, at least include a direct quote from their administrator with their title and a photo.

**3b. The comparison table pricing row is still misleading.** The table shows Caresite at "$149/mo" versus competitors at "Custom quote." While the Basic plan now includes SMS (which fixes the Round 1 feature parity issue), the $149 plan is limited to a single location with 25 AI responses per month. Most administrators comparing against Podium or BirdEye would be looking at the $300 Standard tier for a fair feature comparison. The table should either show a range ("From $149/mo") or show the Standard price, since that is the tier marked "Most Popular."

**3c. No mention of CMS Five-Star ratings.** I flagged this in Round 1 as a missing pain point. Families conflate Google star ratings with CMS Five-Star ratings constantly. A platform built exclusively for skilled nursing should acknowledge this. It is a missed opportunity for differentiation and for connecting with administrators who deal with this confusion daily.

**3d. No mention of referral source impact.** Also flagged in Round 1. Hospital discharge planners and case managers look at Google reviews when recommending facilities. This is where Google reputation directly translates to revenue for SNFs. The pain points and value proposition still do not mention this angle.

**3e. The nav button says "Get Started" while every other CTA says "Book Your Free Audit."** This is a minor inconsistency, but it undermines the otherwise clean CTA alignment. The nav CTA should match the rest of the page.

---

## 4. Top 3 Remaining Changes

### 1. Add a company credibility section with real identity signals

This is still the number one conversion blocker. Add a section between the testimonials and pricing (or between the FAQ and the contact form) that includes: the founder's name and a one-sentence background, how long Caresite has been operating, how many facilities use it (or are in pilot), and whether there is a compliance advisor or healthcare advisory board. If the company is early-stage, own it — "Founded in 2025 by [name], a [background]. Currently serving [X] facilities in [Y] states." Honesty about being early is far better than silence, which reads as evasion.

### 2. Add product screenshots or a visual walkthrough

Show the product. At minimum: one screenshot of the analytics dashboard, one of the SMS campaign setup, and one of the AI dispute assistant in action. Place these in the Features section as supporting visuals for each feature card, or create a dedicated "See It In Action" section between Features and the Comparison Table. If the product is not built yet, use high-fidelity mockups with a "Preview" label. Administrators need to see what they are buying. Every competitor site shows product screenshots — the absence here is conspicuous.

### 3. Make the case study verifiable or reframe it as pilot data

Either name the facility (with permission) and include a direct administrator quote with photo, or reframe the section honestly as "Early Pilot Results" with a note that the facility chose to remain anonymous. Add a before/after Google Business Profile screenshot (blurred facility name if needed, but showing the actual star rating change). The current presentation sits in an uncanny valley — too specific to be hypothetical, too anonymous to be verified. Pick a lane.

---

## Summary

The team addressed four of my five Round 1 priorities: EHR compatibility, CTA alignment, pricing/SMS mismatch, and form depth are all materially better. The page is more honest and more useful than it was. But the single most important issue — company credibility — was not touched, and the testimonials and case study remain unverifiable. For a healthcare buyer, trust is the prerequisite for everything else. The product positioning is strong, the feature set is right, the pricing is reasonable, and the form experience is good. But I still cannot answer the question "Who am I doing business with?" — and until I can, I am not filling out that form.
