# Round 1 Review -- Marcus Chen, Regional Director of Operations
**Persona:** 12-facility SNF chain (GA, AL, TN) | 15 years in LTC ops | Currently on Reputation.com at $450/mo per facility

---

## 1. Would I Send This to My CEO?

Not yet. Close, but not yet.

The page does three things well: it immediately signals SNF-specific focus (the "Built exclusively for skilled nursing facilities" badge is the first thing I see), it names the exact pain points I live with daily, and the comparison table against Reputation.com, Podium, and BirdEye gives me ammunition. Those are the bones of a business case.

What stops me from forwarding the link is that the page talks like it is selling to a single-facility administrator. My CEO does not care about "your facility" -- he cares about "your portfolio." The language throughout is singular. The headline says "Your facility deserves better reviews." The How It Works section says "Upload your discharge list." Singular. I run twelve buildings. I need to know this thing scales before I spend 20 minutes writing an email to leadership.

The second problem: there is no case study with hard dollars. The testimonials are first-name-only with vague facility descriptions. "Rebecca T., Administrator, 120-bed SNF, Ohio" is not something my CEO will treat as evidence. He will ask me "Who is that? Can we call them?" and I will have nothing.

---

## 2. Multi-Facility Concerns

This is the biggest gap on the page. My specific questions:

- **Portfolio dashboard:** The Standard plan says "Multi-location dashboard (up to 10)." I have 12 facilities. So I am immediately pushed into Enterprise at $590/mo per facility. That is $7,080/month, or $84,960/year. Compared to my current $64,800/year on Reputation.com, I need to justify a $20,160 annual increase. The page gives me no tools to do that.
- **Rollout complexity:** Can I pilot with 2-3 facilities and expand? What does the onboarding process look like for a regional chain? Is there a dedicated implementation team, or am I self-serving from a CSV upload screen?
- **Standardization:** Can I enforce consistent campaign settings, response templates, and escalation workflows across all 12 buildings? My administrators have wildly different comfort levels with technology.
- **Regional reporting:** The FAQ mentions "aggregate reporting across all locations" for Enterprise, but that is one line buried in an accordion. Show me a screenshot. Show me what the regional director sees versus what the administrator sees.
- **User roles and permissions:** Who can respond to reviews? Who can approve AI-generated responses before they go live? My compliance officer wants approval authority. Nothing on this page addresses role-based access.

---

## 3. ROI Story

I can start to build one, but the page makes me do too much math myself.

What I can extract:
- "12x average increase in monthly reviews" -- OK, so if I am getting 1 review every 10 months per facility, I would get roughly 12 per month per facility.
- "4.6 avg star rating after 90 days" -- useful, but what is the average starting point? Without a baseline, 4.6 means nothing. If my buildings average 3.1 right now, a jump to 4.6 is extraordinary and I would want proof.
- Dispute assistant: The David N. testimonial says 5 of 7 fake reviews removed. That is compelling, but what is the dollar value of removing a fake 1-star review? The page never connects reviews to census, referrals, or revenue.

What I need but the page does not provide:
- **Revenue impact per star:** The industry data exists. A 2022 NIC study showed that a one-star improvement on Google correlates with a 5-9% increase in census for SNFs in competitive markets. Put that number on the page and let me multiply it by my per-bed-day revenue.
- **Cost comparison calculator:** Let me enter my current spend ($450/mo x 12 facilities = $5,400/mo) and see the delta against Caresite pricing with multi-facility discount applied. Right now I cannot even see what the multi-facility discount is.
- **Time savings:** How many hours per month does the AI response feature save my staff? If my administrators each spend 2 hours/month crafting review responses (they do), and Caresite cuts that to 15 minutes, that is a real number I can put in a business case.

---

## 4. Competitor Switching -- Is There Enough to Leave Reputation.com?

The comparison table is the strongest section on the page for someone like me. Seeing Reputation.com listed with red X marks next to "Built for skilled nursing," "PointClickCare integration," "AI dispute assistant," and "F-tag & survey integration" -- that validates every frustration I have.

But switching cost is real. My concerns:

- **Data migration:** Do I lose my historical review data and analytics from Reputation.com? Can Caresite import it?
- **Google Business Profile transition:** We have 12 connected GBP listings on Reputation.com. What does the cutover look like? Is there downtime in monitoring?
- **Contract timing:** The page says "No annual contracts. No setup fees. Cancel anytime." Great. But it does not address my situation: I am locked into an annual contract with Reputation.com through September. Can I run both in parallel? Is there a bridge pricing plan for the overlap period?
- **Risk of switching:** What if Caresite does not deliver? Reputation.com is mediocre but it is the devil I know. The page has no guarantee, no SLA on review volume increase, and no "we will match your current review velocity or refund" type of commitment.

The comparison table also only shows Caresite with green checks across the board. No product is perfect. The lack of any acknowledged limitations makes me slightly skeptical. What does Reputation.com do better? If the answer is truly "nothing for SNFs," then say that explicitly and explain why.

---

## 5. Compliance Team Buy-In

This is actually the area where the page is strongest. My compliance officer would respond positively to:

- "HIPAA-safe by architecture" with the explanation that no PHI is stored or transmitted
- "No BAA required" -- that is a huge selling point because our legal team bills us every time they have to review a BAA
- The FAQ answer about AI responses never confirming or denying patient relationships
- "Reviewed by healthcare compliance attorneys" (in the FAQ)
- F-tag and survey correlation -- this is the feature that would make my compliance officer an advocate, not just a neutral party

What would concern my compliance officer:

- **The SMS campaigns:** Who owns the phone numbers? Are we sending from Caresite's numbers or ours? What happens when a family replies to the SMS with clinical information or a complaint? The TCPA compliance mention is good, but there is no detail on opt-in/opt-out mechanics or record retention.
- **The AI dispute assistant:** If we file a dispute based on Caresite's AI recommendation and it is wrong, who is liable? Is there a human review step?
- **Data retention:** How long does Caresite retain contact information (names, phone numbers) of discharged residents' family members? What is the data deletion policy?
- **SOC 2 / security posture:** The page never mentions SOC 2, penetration testing, encryption standards, or any security certifications. For a healthcare-adjacent product, this is a notable omission.

---

## 6. Objections My CFO Would Raise

1. **"Why is this more expensive than what we have?"** -- At Enterprise pricing ($590/mo x 12 = $7,080/mo), we are paying $1,680/mo more than Reputation.com. The page says "multi-facility discounts available" but does not show the discount schedule. My CFO will not approve a meeting without knowing the ballpark.
2. **"What is the payback period?"** -- The page claims results in 30 days for volume and 60-90 days for ratings, but does not connect rating improvement to revenue. My CFO needs a payback calculation, not a timeline.
3. **"Who else our size is using this?"** -- The only multi-facility testimonial is "David N., Regional Director of Operations, Multi-facility group, Texas." One testimonial. No facility count, no chain name, no verifiable reference. My CFO will see this as an unproven product.
4. **"What happens if they go under?"** -- Caresite reads like a startup. No information about the company's funding, team size, years in operation, or customer count. My CFO will ask about vendor stability, especially if we are migrating off an established platform.
5. **"Can we negotiate?"** -- The pricing page shows fixed rates. No indication of annual billing discounts, multi-year commitments for better rates, or volume-based pricing tiers. My CFO negotiates everything.

---

## 7. Top 5 Specific Changes (Ranked by Impact on My Buying Decision)

**1. Add a multi-facility pricing calculator or at minimum publish the discount schedule.**
The single most important change. I cannot build a business case without knowing my actual cost. "Contact us for custom pricing" is a friction barrier that kills momentum. At minimum, show tiers: 1-5 facilities, 6-15 facilities, 16-50 facilities, 50+. Even a "starting at" price for each band gives me something to put in an email to my CFO.

**2. Add a ROI calculator that connects star rating improvement to census/revenue.**
Let me input my facility count, average bed count, current star rating, current occupancy rate, and per-bed-day revenue. Output the projected revenue impact of a 0.5-star or 1.0-star improvement. Cite the industry research that supports the correlation. This is the single tool that would let me write a one-page business case in 10 minutes instead of spending a week assembling data.

**3. Rewrite the hero and section headers for multi-facility operators, not single-facility admins.**
Change "Your facility deserves better reviews" to something like "Give every facility in your portfolio a 5-star reputation." Change the How It Works section to acknowledge that regional operators are uploading discharge lists across multiple buildings, not one. Add language about portfolio-level visibility throughout.

**4. Add a verifiable case study from a multi-facility chain.**
One real case study with a named organization (or at least "a 15-facility chain in the Southeast"), specific before/after metrics (review count, star rating, census impact), and a timeline. If the testimonials are real people, offer to connect prospects with them. "We can arrange a reference call" is more powerful than a first-name-only quote.

**5. Add a security and compliance section with SOC 2 status, data retention policy, and SMS compliance details.**
My compliance officer and my CFO both need this. A dedicated "Security & Compliance" page or section that covers SOC 2, data encryption, data retention/deletion, TCPA compliance mechanics, and the AI response approval workflow. This is table stakes for any healthcare-adjacent vendor selling to multi-facility operators.

---

## Summary

The product positioning is excellent. This is clearly built by people who understand the SNF space. The pain points section and comparison table are strong. The HIPAA-safe architecture story is differentiated and credible.

But the page is selling to a 1-facility administrator making a $149/mo decision. I am trying to make an $85K/year decision for a 12-facility chain. The page does not meet me where I am. Give me the multi-facility story, the ROI math, and one solid reference, and I will have this on my CEO's desk by end of week.
