# Round 1 Review: Diane Marshall

**Reviewer:** Diane Marshall, Administrator, 180-bed SNF, Cleveland, OH
**Experience:** 22 years in post-acute care
**Situation:** Facility at 2.8 Google rating after two unfair reviews. Admissions director reports families choosing competitors. Actively evaluating reputation management tools.

---

## 1. First Impression

The headline "Your facility deserves better reviews" lands immediately. I felt seen within the first three seconds -- that is exactly the conversation I have been having with my admissions director. The badge "Built exclusively for skilled nursing facilities" is what kept me scrolling instead of bouncing. Every other platform I have looked at this month (Podium, BirdEye) opens with a generic pitch about "businesses" and I have to hunt around to figure out if they even know what an SNF is.

The three stat cards below the headline are strong -- 12x review increase and 4.6 average rating after 90 days are the kind of numbers I would bring to my regional VP to justify the spend. "100% HIPAA compliant by architecture" is a bold claim and I want to believe it, but I will come back to that under credibility.

One concern right away: the page feels like it is talking to a single-facility operator. I run one building, but my regional director oversees nine. If she were the one landing here, I am not sure the hero would speak to her. The multi-facility story gets buried down in pricing tiers.

---

## 2. Credibility

This is where I start hesitating. A few things that build trust and a few that erode it:

**What works:**
- The testimonials use specific numbers ("from two Google reviews to fourteen in our first month," "3.2 to 4.4 in 90 days," "Five of seven were taken down"). These feel real, not like marketing copy.
- The comparison table against Podium, BirdEye, and Reputation.com is helpful. Naming the competitors directly is a confidence signal.
- The FAQ answer on HIPAA is well-written and correctly distinguishes between "HIPAA compliant" and "HIPAA-safe by architecture." Someone on this team actually understands the distinction.

**What is missing:**
- No real company information. Who is behind Caresite? There is no About page, no team section, no founding story. I am being asked to connect my discharge workflows to a platform and I do not know if this is two people in a garage or a funded company with a support team. This is a dealbreaker for me.
- No logos or named facilities in the testimonials. "Rebecca T., Administrator, 120-bed SNF, Ohio" -- that could be anyone. If I cannot verify a single customer, the testimonials lose most of their weight. I would need at least one named facility or recognizable group.
- No mention of how long the company has been operating or how many facilities use it.
- No mention of any third-party security review, SOC 2, or independent compliance assessment. "HIPAA-safe by architecture" is a smart framing, but my compliance officer will ask who validated that claim.
- The footer links to Privacy Policy and Terms of Service but those appear to be placeholder links. If I click those and get a 404, I am closing the tab.

---

## 3. Value Clarity

I understand the core value proposition well: Caresite automates review solicitation after discharge via SMS, gates sentiment so bad experiences go to a private form instead of Google, and provides AI tools to respond to and dispute reviews. That is clear and I can explain it to my boss.

**Pricing clarity -- mixed:**
- I appreciate that prices are published. After spending two weeks playing phone tag with Podium's sales team for a quote, seeing "$149 / $300 / $590 per facility per month" is refreshing.
- However, the Basic plan at $149/mo does not include SMS campaigns -- only email. That is a problem because the entire pitch above is built around SMS being the game-changer. The feature that the hero section sells (SMS campaigns that turn discharged families into five-star advocates) requires the $300/mo Standard plan. That feels like a bait-and-switch, even if it is unintentional.
- "$590/mo per facility" for Enterprise is steep. For my 180-bed building, that is $7,080/year. I could justify it if I saw hard ROI data (e.g., "each additional star is worth X admissions per month"), but there is no ROI calculator or financial justification anywhere on the page.
- The comparison table shows Caresite at "$149/mo" vs. competitors at "Custom quote." But $149 is the plan without SMS. The honest comparison would show $300/mo since that is the tier with feature parity.

---

## 4. Pain Point Resonance

The three pain points described match my reality almost exactly:

- **"You're invisible online"** -- Yes. We had three Google reviews total before last quarter. One was from 2019. My competitor down the road has 47 reviews and a 4.1 rating, and their care is not better than ours.
- **"Negative reviews go unchallenged"** -- This is my exact situation. Two reviews dropped us to 2.8. One is from a family member who was upset about a billing issue that had nothing to do with care quality. The other may be from a competitor's employee. I have no idea how to get either removed.
- **"Generic tools don't understand compliance"** -- This resonates, but it is the weakest of the three. Most administrators I know are not thinking about HIPAA risk from review responses -- they are just not responding at all because they do not know what to say. The real pain is paralysis, not compliance risk. The compliance angle is important, but it lands more with DONs and compliance officers than with administrators making the purchase decision.

**What is missing from the pain points:**
- No mention of CMS Five-Star ratings and how Google reviews interact with (or are confused with) the CMS star system. Families conflate the two constantly, and that is a real pain point.
- No mention of the referral source angle. Hospital discharge planners and case managers absolutely look at Google reviews when recommending facilities to families. That is where the real revenue impact is for me.

---

## 5. Feature Gaps

Things I would need to see before buying:

- **CMS Five-Star integration or at least acknowledgment.** My CMS rating and my Google rating tell two different stories. I need a platform that helps me manage both narratives, or at least understands that families see both.
- **Reporting I can show to my board or regional VP.** The "analytics dashboard" is mentioned but I have no screenshots, no sample reports, no idea what it looks like. I need to present data to people above me to justify this spend.
- **Family portal or response tracking.** When someone leaves negative feedback through the private form, what happens next? Who gets notified? Is there a workflow? The page does not explain the recovery side at all.
- **Integration with other EHRs.** PointClickCare is mentioned prominently, but my facility runs MatrixCare. Am I out of luck? This is not addressed anywhere.
- **Google Business Profile management.** Can I respond to reviews directly from Caresite, or do I still have to log into Google separately? This is unclear.
- **Onboarding and implementation details.** How long does setup take? Do I need IT involvement? Will my staff need training? The "How It Works" section makes it sound like three easy steps, but I have been through enough software implementations to know it is never that simple.

---

## 6. Objections

What would stop me from filling out the contact form right now:

1. **I do not know who this company is.** No team page, no company history, no indication of size or stability. I am not connecting my discharge data to an unknown entity.
2. **I cannot verify a single customer.** Anonymous testimonials from "Rebecca T." are not enough. Give me a name I can call.
3. **My facility uses MatrixCare, not PointClickCare.** The page does not tell me if that matters.
4. **The form asks for almost nothing.** Name, email, facility name -- that is it. No phone number, no bed count, no current rating, no EHR selection. This tells me either the sales process is very lightweight (good) or no one is going to follow up with anything relevant to my situation (bad). I would actually feel more confident if the form asked smarter questions.
5. **"Start Your Free Trial" but no details on the trial.** How long is it? What is included? Do I need a credit card? The FAQ does not cover this. The small print says "No credit card required" but that is it.

---

## 7. Top 5 Specific Changes (Ranked by Importance)

### 1. Add a company credibility section -- About, team, founding story, customer count
This is the single biggest barrier to conversion for someone in my position. I need to know who I am doing business with. Add a brief section above the footer with founder names, a company description, and a concrete customer count (even "trusted by 50+ facilities" would help). If there is a healthcare advisory board or compliance advisor, name them. This is non-negotiable for a healthcare buyer.

### 2. Fix the pricing/feature mismatch between the hero pitch and the Basic plan
The hero sells SMS campaigns. The Basic plan does not include SMS. Either add SMS to Basic (even at a lower volume), or change the hero copy to be honest about which tier delivers the promised experience. Right now, someone who clicks "Start Free Trial" expecting SMS-driven reviews will feel misled when they see the Basic tier is email-only. Alternatively, default-highlight the Standard plan in the hero CTA so expectations are set correctly.

### 3. Add a "Results" or "Case Study" section with verifiable outcomes
Replace or supplement the testimonials with at least one detailed case study: facility name (with permission), starting rating, ending rating, timeframe, number of reviews generated, and any measurable impact on admissions or referrals. Include a before/after screenshot of a Google Business Profile. Administrators share case studies with their leadership -- give us something we can forward in an email.

### 4. Address EHR compatibility beyond PointClickCare
Add a short section or FAQ entry that lists supported EHRs or explains the CSV upload path for facilities not on PointClickCare. MatrixCare, American HealthTech, and Netsmart are all common in skilled nursing. If CSV upload works for any EHR, say that clearly -- it is actually a selling point, not a limitation.

### 5. Expand the contact form and add a phone number
Add fields for phone number, number of facilities, current Google rating, and EHR system. This lets the sales team personalize the first call and shows the prospect that Caresite understands the qualification questions that matter. Also add a direct phone number somewhere on the page -- even if it goes to voicemail. Healthcare administrators over 40 (which is most of us) want to know there is a human we can call.

---

## Summary

Caresite's positioning is strong. The SNF-only angle is genuinely differentiated and the pain points are accurate. The product features described are exactly what I need. But the page is asking me to trust an unknown company with a connection to my discharge workflows, and it does not give me enough evidence to take that leap. The credibility gap is the primary conversion blocker. Fix that, align the pricing presentation with the hero promise, and give me one verifiable case study, and I would fill out the form today.
