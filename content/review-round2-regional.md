# Round 2 Review -- Marcus Chen, Regional Director of Operations
**Persona:** 12-facility SNF chain (GA, AL, TN) | 15 years in LTC ops | Currently on Reputation.com at $450/mo per facility

---

## 1. What Improved

The contact form now asks for number of facilities (1, 2-5, 6-15, 16-50, 50+) and role (including "Regional Operations"), which signals that someone on the team heard the multi-facility feedback. That is a real improvement. When I fill out that form and select "6-15 facilities" and "Regional Operations," I at least feel like the intake process recognizes my buyer profile.

The case study section is stronger than what I remember being described in Round 1 feedback. There is now a concrete before/after block: 2.8 stars to 4.4, 0.1 reviews/month to 14, setup time of 15 minutes. Those are specific, measurable numbers. The "5 policy-violating reviews removed via AI dispute assistant" and "92% of negative feedback captured privately" callouts give me data points I can actually cite in an internal memo.

The comparison table remains the strongest section on the page. It has not regressed and still provides the competitive ammunition I need against Reputation.com, Podium, and BirdEye. The "Pricing transparency" row showing "$149/mo" against three "Custom quote" entries is a smart move.

The FAQ now explicitly addresses multi-facility usage ("The Standard plan supports up to 10 locations on a single dashboard, and the Enterprise plan supports unlimited locations"). That answer did not exist in the form I reviewed last time, or at least was not this clear.

## 2. Still Missing

There is no way for me to input my facility count, current spend, bed count, or occupancy rate and see a projected return. The page still forces me to do all the math myself. I said last time that I need to build a business case in 10 minutes, not a week. Nothing has changed on this front.

**No multi-facility pricing schedule.** The pricing section still shows three flat-rate tiers ($149, $300, $590 per facility per month) with a single line at the bottom: "Multi-facility discounts available. Contact us for custom pricing." This was my #1 priority in Round 1. I need to know what 12 facilities costs before I contact anyone. My CFO will not approve a discovery call without a ballpark number. Even a simple table showing 1-5, 6-15, 16-50, 50+ facility bands with approximate per-facility pricing would solve this.

**No product visuals.** There are zero screenshots, mockups, or UI previews anywhere on the page. I am being asked to evaluate a $85K/year software purchase based entirely on text descriptions. Show me the dashboard. Show me the multi-location view. Show me what an AI-generated review response looks like. Show me the dispute assistant workflow. Every competitor in this space shows their product. Caresite shows nothing.

**Hero and section headers still use singular language.** The headline is still "Your facility deserves better reviews." The Features header still says "Everything your facility needs to own its reputation." The How It Works section still says "Upload your discharge list" (singular). The CTA banner says "Ready to see your facility's growth plan?" I flagged this in Round 1 as change #3. None of it has been updated for multi-facility buyers.

**Testimonials are still unverifiable.** Rebecca T., David N., Maria S. -- first-name-only, no organization, no facility count for David beyond "Multi-facility group, Texas." My CEO will not treat these as evidence. I asked for verifiable case studies and reference call availability in Round 1. Neither exists.

**No security/compliance detail section.** Round 1, change #5: SOC 2 status, data retention policy, SMS compliance mechanics, AI response approval workflow. None of this has been added. The HIPAA-safe messaging is still strong, but it is not enough for a procurement process at a 12-facility chain. My compliance officer and IT director both need to see encryption standards, data retention/deletion policies, and security certifications.

---

## 3. New Issues

**The case study facility is anonymous and single-site.** The new case study describes "A 120-bed skilled nursing facility in the Pacific Northwest." That is a single facility. As a regional operator evaluating this for 12 buildings, I want to see a case study from a multi-facility chain. What happens when you roll this out across 8 or 15 facilities? Do the results scale linearly? Are there regional rollout challenges? The single-site case study is helpful but does not address my primary buying concern.

**The CTA banner mid-page feels premature.** The CTABanner component sits between the case study and the features section. At that point in my scroll, I have not yet seen the feature list, comparison table, pricing, or FAQ. Asking me to "Book Your Free Audit" before I have seen the full value proposition is a wasted impression. For a buyer at my level, the CTA after pricing or after the FAQ would be more effective.

**No mention of onboarding, implementation, or rollout support.** The Enterprise tier mentions "Dedicated account manager," but there is nothing about implementation timelines, training, onboarding for multiple facilities, or what the first 30 days look like operationally. When I am rolling out a new tool across 12 buildings with administrators who have different technology comfort levels, I need to know there is a structured onboarding process. This is a procurement objection that the page does not preempt.

**The contact form success message says "get your facility set up" (singular).** Even the post-submission experience uses singular language. If I just told you I have 6-15 facilities, acknowledge that in the confirmation. "A member of our team will reach out within one business day to discuss your multi-facility rollout" would be more appropriate.

---

## 4. Top 3 Remaining Changes

**1. Add a visible multi-facility pricing table or calculator directly in the Pricing section.**
Stop hiding behind "Contact us for custom pricing." Publish pricing bands: for example, 1 facility at list price, 2-5 facilities at 15% off, 6-15 at 25% off, 16-50 at 35% off. Even approximate ranges are better than nothing. I will not forward a page to my CEO that cannot answer the question "What does this cost us?" My CFO will reject the conversation before it starts. This has been the #1 ask for two review rounds and it is still missing.

**2. Add at least 3-4 product screenshots or UI mockups showing the dashboard, multi-location view, AI response interface, and dispute assistant.**
I am evaluating software I have never seen. No screenshots, no demo video, no interactive preview. Every section describes capabilities in text but never shows them. A single screenshot of the multi-location dashboard would do more to sell a regional operator than three paragraphs of feature descriptions. If the product is not built yet, show annotated wireframes. If it is built, there is no reason not to show it.

**3. Replace singular "facility" language with portfolio/multi-facility language in the hero, section headers, and CTA copy.**
The hero should speak to portfolio operators: "Give every facility in your portfolio a 5-star reputation" or "One platform for every building in your network." The How It Works section should acknowledge batch uploads across multiple facilities. The CTA banner should say "your portfolio" not "your facility." The post-submission message should reference the facility count I just selected. This is a copy pass that could be done in an afternoon and would immediately signal that Caresite understands buyers at my level.

---

## Summary

The page has improved incrementally. The case study metrics, contact form segmentation, and FAQ clarity are better than Round 1. But the three structural gaps I flagged -- multi-facility pricing transparency, ROI tooling, and product visuals -- remain completely unaddressed. These are not polish items. They are the difference between me forwarding this link to my CEO and closing the tab.

I am still not ready to send this up the chain. Give me the pricing bands, show me the product, and talk to me like someone managing a portfolio, and we can have a real conversation.
